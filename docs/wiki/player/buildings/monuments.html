Monuments

Overview

Monuments are large multi-phase construction projects. Many campaign missions require a Monuments rating high enough to finish the city; some also demand a specific monument type. Construction depends on raw materials (bricks, stone, timber, and later marble or sandstone), Work Camps for peasant labour, and the right construction guilds to direct the work.

Detailed pages for individual monuments will appear here as they are documented. Currently: Sphinx.

Size and materials charts adapted from Pharaoh Heaven — Monuments and the Pharaoh/Cleopatra Monuments Chart.

How construction works

Place site → Stock materials → Work Camps + guilds → Phases / courses → Finished monument
  1. Place the footprint on clear ground (or cliffs / rock where the monument requires it).
  2. Deliver materials to storage yards near the site. Quarries and brickworks are often the bottleneck.
  3. Staff Work Camps so labourers walk to the monument. The same camps also serve farms — harvest season can starve construction.
  4. Staff the required guilds (bricklayers, stonemasons, carpenters, and in Cleopatra artisans). Guild members supervise on site.
  5. Work proceeds in phases (mastaba courses, pyramid courses, scaffolding, finishing). Progress shows in the monument info window.
Monuments do not need a road connection. Place them where materials and labour can reach them reasonably quickly — not across the entire map from your quarries and camps.

Guilds & labour

BuildingRole
Work Camp Peasant labour for clearing, hauling, and building courses. Shared with floodplain farming.
Bricklayers’ Guild Brick mastabas and brick-core pyramids.
Stonemasons’ Guild Stone tombs, stepped / true / bent pyramids, sphinx, obelisks, mausoleum, sun temple, most Cleopatra monuments.
Carpenters’ Guild Wooden scaffolding and ramps (pyramids, sphinx, mausoleum, obelisks, etc.).
Artisans’ Guild Cleo Painting and finishing for royal burial tombs and several Cleopatra monuments.

You often need more than one guild of the same type on large monuments. Keep storage yards supplied; idle guilds with empty yards do nothing.

Cleopatra monuments

The Cleopatra expansion adds cliff tombs and Alexandria-era monuments. They use sandstone or marble, often need the Artisans’ Guild, and some must sit on cliffs or a special island.

MonumentFootprintNotes
Royal Burial Tomb Cleo 11×20 … 23×29 Cut into cliff; lamps + paint; Artisans + Work Camps
Abu Simbel Cleo 9×21 Entirely on cliffs; four colossi; Stonemasons + Artisans
Great Library of Alexandria Cleo 13×14 Marble + timber; papyrus supply; Artisans
Pharos Lighthouse Cleo 6×6 Marble; three-tier tower on Pharos Island
Caesareum Cleo 15×15 Marble + sandstone; temple complex with obelisks

Monument rating

Finished (and partially built) monuments contribute to the city’s Monuments rating. In Akhenaten the score is approximate and additive: each monument type has a weight in src/scripts/city/monuments.js, then rating ≈ 2.25 × Σ(weight × progress) + 4.5. Exact original per-monument points are still a remake task (F3). Examples with current weights: one finished small mastaba → 9; three → 18; one medium stepped pyramid → 40.

Tips

  • Start material production early — stone and bricks take longer than the build phases themselves.
  • Build one monument first; when a course finishes and labour frees up, start the next.
  • During floodplain harvest, expect Work Camp labour to thin out; stockpile stone beforehand.
  • Place storage yards and guilds near the site so cart pushers and guild walkers waste less time.
  • Import granite, limestone, or sandstone when the map has no matching quarry.
