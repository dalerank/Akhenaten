Briefing

The mission opens with the history panel (message_history_serabit_khadim) and the mission briefing popup (message_mission_serabit_khadim):

Like Den before him, Pharaoh Huni has ordered an expedition into the harsh land of Sinai to acquire turquoise gemstones and copper. He wishes you to lead this expedition, to a place called Serabit Khadim, where the building remains of an earlier Egyptian outpost may still be found. Their current condition is unknown, but they may still provide some means of defense for the expedition.

You should be aware that the last such expedition sent to this area never returned. A later expedition sent to recover them also did not return. Nevertheless, if we are to arm our soldiers adequately, our Kingdom needs copper for making weapons, and this metal is scarce in our land.

Prepare yourself, for you will be under constant threat of attack from the Bedouin of the Sinai desert, and from our enemies the Canaanites. Mine what copper and gemstones you may under these conditions, and be prompt in fulfilling Pharaoh's requests. You may employ jewelers to use any surplus gemstones to fashion jewelry for the people occupying the settlement.

Serabit el-Khadim is a real plateau in the southwest Sinai peninsula where the ancient Egyptians mined turquoise from the Old Kingdom onward. A temple of Hathor, "Lady of Turquoise", stood at the site, and the Proto-Sinaitic inscriptions discovered there by Flinders Petrie's 1905 expedition are among the earliest known alphabetic writing. In the campaign this is the first mission of the Old Kingdom block: a hostile desert outpost with no Nile floodplain, sustained by mining, hunting, and caravan trade.

Objectives

  • Population 2 000 citizens
  • Kingdom rating 80

There are no culture, prosperity, or monument goals. The kingdom rating requirement of 80 is the real difficulty: every one of Pharaoh's requests refused or failed costs reputation, while each fulfilled request raises it. Keeping copper, gems, weapons, and luxury goods flowing is the core of the mission.

Scripted events

All events are driven by the mission script — requests arrive on a fixed yearly schedule, and two scripted invasions land in the early years. Invasion timing follows the Pharaoh Heaven walkthrough.

Year 1 Pharaoh requests 8 copper (12 months to comply). Fulfilling it raises kingdom rating by 8; refusing costs 6.
Year 2 Bedouin raid — a foreign army of 8 units invades and tries to destroy buildings. Pharaoh also requests 6 gemstones.
Year 3 Pharaoh requests 8 weapons. In midsummer a Canaanite invasion of 10 units arrives — stronger than the Bedouin raid.
Year 4 Pharaoh requests 4 luxury goods — surplus gems must be turned into jewelry at the Jewels Workshop.

Strategy

Serabit Khadim is a desert survival mission: no floodplain farming, hostile raids from year 2, and a kingdom rating goal that punishes a stalled economy. Everything revolves around the two mines and the defense of the settlement.

Phase 1 — Settle and feed

Food comes from Hunting Lodges — ostrich herds roam the map. Place housing near the road, cover it with Water Supply, Firehouse, Architect's Post, and Police Station, and set up two or more Hunting Lodges with a Granary and Bazaar. There is no Nilometer here; water and food are scarce, so keep the housing block compact.

Phase 2 — Mines and industry

Copper Mines and Gemstone Mines are the reason this expedition exists. Site them on the ore-bearing rock, staff them via Work Camps, and store output in Storage Yards. A Weaponsmith turns copper into weapons (needed for Pharaoh's year-3 request and for your own recruits); the Jewels Workshop turns gems into luxury goods for the year-4 request and for housing evolution. Mines deplete over time — pace your exports and keep a reserve for requests.

Phase 3 — Defense

The first Bedouin raid arrives around year 2, Canaanites from year 3. Build a Recruiter, a Weaponsmith, and at least one Infantry Fort and one Archers Fort before the second year. Mud walls, gatehouses, and towers are available — wall off the approach to the housing block and mines. The old outpost ruins on the map hint at the defensive line. Raids continue through the mission, so do not disband the army after the first victory.

Phase 4 — Trade and the kingdom rating

Land routes to Abu, Behdet, and the Selima Oasis let you import what the desert cannot provide (barley, beer, linen, timber, papyrus) and export copper, gems, and jewelry. Fulfill every request from Pharaoh — each one completed is +8 kingdom rating, and 80 is required to win. If the rating stalls, send gifts to Pharaoh from personal savings.

Tips

  • Requests arrive on a fixed schedule (copper → gems → weapons → luxury goods, one per year). Stockpile the next resource in advance instead of scrambling when the request lands.
  • Keep 8 copper in a Storage Yard before the end of year 1 — the first request has a 12-month deadline.
  • Ostrich herds respawn from two points in the north of the map; keep Hunting Lodges within range of both.
  • Temples of Seth keep the god of the desert appeased — his wrath in a military mission is costly. Ra's blessing helps trade.
  • Scribal School and entertainment (Booth → Pavilion chain) are available and needed to push housing high enough for 2 000 population in a compact block.

Developer reference

Collapsed sections below show exactly where each part of this mission lives in the source tree. All paths are relative to the repository root.

Mission script — src/scripts/mission/m_011_serabit_khadim.js

The entire mission — configuration block, variables, and all event handlers — lives in one file:

src/scripts/mission/m_011_serabit_khadim.js

This file is imported by src/scripts/missions.js. The mission11 { … } block at the top is the static configuration parsed at startup:

// lines 3 – 130  (m_011_serabit_khadim.js)
mission11 {
  start_message : "message_mission_serabit_khadim"
  player_rank : 1

  choice [                       // post-victory branch: Buhen (13) / Dahshur (14)
    { name : "Buhen",   id : 13 }
    { name : "Dahshur", id : 14 }
  ]

  initial_funds [7500, 5000, 3750, 2500, 2000]  // Very Easy … Very Hard

  win_criteria {
    population { enabled : true, goal : 2000 }
    kingdom    { enabled : true, goal : 80 }
    // culture, prosperity, monuments — disabled
  }

  env { has_animals : true, hide_nilometer : true }
}

Goal values follow the Pharaoh Heaven / Impressions walkthroughs (pop 2000, kingdom 80). Trade partners with active routes: Abu, Behdet, Selima Oasis. Men-nefer and Kebet are declared in cities[] but their sells/buys lists are currently commented out (lines 76–91).

Event handlers — all functions in m_011_serabit_khadim.js

Each function is bound to a game event via an attribute annotation. Handlers fire only while mission=mission11 is active. All invasions use the JS API city.start_foreign_army_invasion (not EVENT_TYPE_INVASION).

  • event_mission_start line 133 Shows the start message, enables the empire map, and unlocks every advisor up to Diplomacy. mission11_on_start
  • city_animals / create_herds line 142 Registers two ostrich herd spawn points (5 ostriches each, radius 16) at tiles (55, 75) and (85, 135). mission11_register_animals
  • event_advance_month (request 1) line 153 From year 1: request for 8 copper, 12 months. Completion +8 kingdom, refusal −6 (chain events tags 101/102). Runs once. mission11_pharaoh_request_copper
  • event_advance_month (request 2) line 172 From year 2: request for 6 gems, 12 months. +8 / −6 kingdom (tags 201/202). Runs once. mission11_pharaoh_request_gems
  • event_advance_month (request 3) line 191 From year 3: request for 8 weapons, 12 months. +8 / −6 kingdom (tags 301/302). Runs once. mission11_pharaoh_request_weapons
  • event_advance_month (request 4) line 210 From year 4: request for 4 luxury goods, 12 months. +8 / −6 kingdom (tags 401/402). Runs once. mission11_pharaoh_request_luxury_goods
  • event_advance_month (invasion 1) line 230 From year 2: Bedouin raid — start_foreign_army_invasion(enemy: ENEMY_0_BARBARIAN, size: 8, want_destroy_buildings: 8). Runs once. mission11_bedouin_invasion
  • event_advance_month (invasion 2) line 247 From year 3, month 6: Canaanite invasion — start_foreign_army_invasion(enemy: ENEMY_2_CANAANITE, size: 10, want_destroy_buildings: 10). Runs once. mission11_canaanite_invasion

Save file and map data

mission1.pak (scenario offset for id 11 — see GamestateIO::get_campaign_scenario_offset, src/io/gamestate/boilerplate.cpp:120)
