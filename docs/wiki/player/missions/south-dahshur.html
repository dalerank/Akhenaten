Briefing

The mission opens with the history panel (message_history_south_dahshur) and the briefing popup (message_mission_south_dahshur):

Our new Pharaoh, Snofru, is determined that this, the fourth dynasty, be remembered as the greatest ever to rule Egypt. His architects have contrived a tomb even more spectacular than the stepped pyramid of Huni, and Pharaoh wishes you to oversee its construction. You will need to establish a fair-sized settlement south of Dahshur, the site for Pharaoh's bent pyramid.

The bent pyramid is to be constructed of a plain stone core, and faced with fine white limestone, that it may forever shine under the desert sun. You will find sufficient quantities of limestone at Dahshur, but you'll need to import the necessary amount of plain stone.

Precious gemstones may be obtained from a recently established Egyptian outpost at Serabit Khadim, in the land of Sinai. Lately the supply has become erratic, due to unrelenting Bedouin and Canaanite attacks.

Dahshur is the royal necropolis south of Saqqara where Snofru built the Bent Pyramid — so called because its angle changes partway up, a mid-construction correction that makes it the transitional monument between step and true pyramids. In the campaign this is the builder branch of the Snofru fork: a peaceful construction mission where the whole city exists to feed the pyramid stone and limestone.

The bent pyramid is not implemented yet (task C4); a medium stepped pyramid stands in, which produces a rating of 25, so the temporary goal is 25. When the bent pyramid is implemented the goal reverts to 21 and the stepped pyramid is swapped for it.

Scripted events

South Dahshur is a peaceful construction mission — no invasions. Pharaoh's requests follow the walkthrough order (grain, limestone, beer); years and amounts are provisional until verified.

Strategy

Everything here serves the pyramid. Culture is not scored, so effort goes into food (for 3 500 people), the stone supply chain, and keeping prosperity and kingdom ratings healthy while the monument consumes labor for years.

Phase 1 — Food and settlement

Grain and fig farms on the floodplain plus Water Lifts and Irrigation Ditches support a large population. Build out housing early — 3 500 citizens is the largest target so far and provides the workforce the pyramid needs.

Phase 2 — Stone and limestone

Limestone is quarried locally (Limestone Quarry) for the pyramid's facing; plain stone for the core must be imported — Men-nefer and Abu both sell it. Set up Storage Yards next to the pyramid site and run Work Camps plus the three construction guilds. Export surplus pottery and limestone for income.

Phase 3 — The pyramid

Lay out the medium pyramid early — it is the win condition and takes years of stone deliveries. Keep the stone import steady; if the core supply stalls the phases stall with it. Fulfil Pharaoh's grain/limestone/beer requests along the way to hold kingdom rating at 50.

Tips

  • Import plain stone in bulk before starting the upper phases — a full Storage Yard prevents construction stalls.
  • No army is required, but keep a token guard; a "siege elsewhere in Egypt" can raise trade-route upkeep, so watch the treasury.
  • Prosperity 25 comes from housing evolution — import gems from Serabit Khadim and craft jewelry to raise home values.
