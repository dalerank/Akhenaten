Briefing

The mission opens with the history panel (message_history_rostja) and the briefing popup (message_mission_giza):

Pharaoh Khufu has at last made his plans known. You have been awarded the status of Nomarch, but also charged with carrying out the most ambitious building project ever undertaken in our land.

Pharaoh's eternal resting place shall be a massive pyramid complex on the plateau outside Rostja. His sarcophagus shall be of solid granite, his funeral barge of precious Lebanese cedar. Beside it, a smaller pyramid shall be built for his son prince Khafra, who also declares his image be carved into the living rock as a "sphinx" — the body of a lion and the head of a man.

You'll need a large settlement to support the effort. Conditions there may not be overly refined; your goal is only to complete these three great projects, and to honor Pharaoh — whatever the cost.

Rostja (Rosetau) is the ancient name for the Giza plateau. This is the campaign's marquee mission — Khufu's Great Pyramid, Khafra's pyramid, and the Sphinx — a pure construction-and-loyalty scenario with no population, culture, or prosperity requirement, only monuments and the Pharaoh's favor.

  • Monument rating33 — build the full available monument set

Temporary deviation from the original: the original requires a Sphinx + a pyramid complex + a (prince's) pyramid, for monument rating 53. True pyramids (task C3) and the Sphinx (task C6) are not implemented, and the available monuments cannot reach 53. As a stand-in the mission requires the full available set — both stepped pyramids (8 + 16) and both mastabas (2 + 2), summing to a rating of 33. When C3 and C6 land, the goal reverts to 53 with the pyramid complex, prince's pyramid, and Sphinx.

Strategy

Rostja is all about the monuments. There is no population, culture, or prosperity goal — the entire city exists to feed stone, limestone, and granite to the great projects while keeping the Pharaoh (kingdom rating 50) and the gods content, and repelling two Libyan raids.

Phase 1 — Workforce and stone

Build enough housing to staff a large labor pool (even without a population goal), then stand up Stone, Limestone, and Granite Quarries with multiple Work Camps and all three guilds. Import extra plain stone from Men-nefer, limestone from On, and Lebanese cedar (timber) from Byblos for the ramps and the funeral barge.

Phase 2 — The great projects

Lay out all four monuments early (both stepped pyramids and both mastabas stand in for the pyramid complex, prince's pyramid, and Sphinx). They take years — keep the stone/limestone pipeline saturated so no phase stalls. The generous starting funds (up to 10 000 Db) exist because you'll be buying a great deal of material.

Phase 3 — Defense

Two Libyan raids (years 3 and 6) come from the east/south-east. Wall the settlement and keep a standing academy-trained army; the monuments themselves are the prize the raiders target.

Tips

  • Ignore culture and prosperity — pour everything into the stone economy and the four monuments.
  • Keep large limestone and plain-stone buffers; a stall in supply freezes construction for months.
  • Each fulfilled Pharaoh request is +8 kingdom toward the goal of 50 — the requests are the fastest path there.
