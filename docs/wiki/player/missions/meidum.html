Briefing

The mission opens with the history panel (message_history_meidum) and the mission briefing popup (message_mission_meidum):

Pharaoh Huni wishes to spend everlasting eternity in a stepped pyramid, like that of Djoser before him. He wishes to be surrounded by the tombs of his nobles, and has chosen Meidum, in Lower Egypt, as the site for this royal necropolis.

As a symbol of thanks for the many generations of faithful service provided by your house, Huni has also consented to allow you to be interred at Meidum, in your own tomb. In doing this, he has also conferred great honor upon your family.

To ensure that the wisdom and learning of Egypt is preserved through the ages, Pharaoh Huni also urges the construction of royal libraries. Once filled with scrolls of papyrus, these will provide higher education to the upper classes.

Pharaoh has sent another of his faithful courtiers on an expedition to Serabit Khadim, in the harsh land of Sinai, to acquire turquoise gemstones. If this expedition is a success, you may look forward to importing gemstones from there. Jewelers can use these to fashion jewelry, a valuable luxury good, for the people of your city.

The once-thriving city of Behdet has begun to decline, and no longer exports many of the commodities they were once known for.

Meidum lies on the desert edge of Lower Egypt near the Faiyum. The historical pyramid of Meidum — begun as a step pyramid for Huni and later converted by Snofru into the first attempt at a true pyramid — partially collapsed in antiquity, leaving the strange three-stepped tower that stands today. In the campaign this is the necropolis-building alternative to the Serabit Khadim mining expedition: the first mission where monument construction, libraries, and higher-class housing all come together.

Objectives

  • Population 3 000 citizens
  • Culture rating 25
  • Prosperity rating 25
  • Monument rating 31 — complete both the small and the medium stepped pyramid
  • Kingdom rating 40
Temporary deviation from the original: the original mission requires a small stepped pyramid plus a stepped pyramid complex (monument rating 39). The complex is not implemented yet; until it is, the goal is 31 and the medium stepped pyramid stands in for the complex. See the Developer Reference for the tracking TODO.

Scripted events

Pharaoh's requests follow the walkthrough order — wood first in year 3, then one request per year. Amounts and later years are provisional until verified against the original mission data.

Year 3 Pharaoh requests 10 wood (12 months). Fulfilling raises kingdom rating by 8; refusing costs 6 — this applies to every request below.
Year 4 Pharaoh requests 10 reeds. In midsummer the Libyans invade from the east (12 units, preceded by a warning message) — the walkthrough places their landing on the marshland island.
Year 5 Pharaoh requests 10 pottery.
Year 6 Pharaoh requests 15 papyrus.
Year 7 Pharaoh requests 20 grain.
Year 8 Pharaoh requests 10 plain stone.

Strategy

Meidum is the first full monument economy: two stepped pyramids demand a steady flow of plain stone, work camps, and all three construction guilds, while the culture goal requires libraries and a broad service net. Papyrus is the main export income.

Phase 1 — Food and papyrus

Floodplain grain and fig farms feed the city; reed gatherers in the marshes supply the Papyrus Workshop. Papyrus does double duty — it is your primary trade export (Men-nefer and the Selima Oasis buy it) and it stocks the libraries needed for the culture rating. Beware the hippos in the marshland when routing gatherers.

Phase 2 — The necropolis

Lay out the small and medium stepped pyramids early — they take years to finish. Stone Quarries plus a Work Camp and the Stonemasons' Guild drive construction; the Carpenters' and Bricklayers' Guilds are needed for the early phases. Keep a dedicated Storage Yard for plain stone next to the sites. Mastabas for the nobles can be added alongside — they contribute a little to the monument rating but are not required for the goal.

Phase 3 — Defense of the island

Around year 4 the Libyans land in the east. Build a Recruiter and Weaponsmith in advance and keep one company of infantry and one of archers ready; a Transport Wharf lets you ferry troops across the water to meet the landing (the walkthrough's recommended tactic), and warships can intercept along the river. The invasion is a raid, not a siege — a modest standing army suffices.

Phase 4 — Culture and prosperity

Culture 25 needs schools, libraries, temples, and entertainment coverage; prosperity 25 needs housing beyond the basic levels — import gems from Serabit Khadim, craft jewelry, and let the luxury goods raise home values. Hold festivals for Osiris and Ptah to keep the gods content while the pyramids consume the city's labor.

Tips

  • Start both pyramids as soon as the stone chain works — the monument rating only reaches the goal when both are fully complete.
  • Wood is requested in year 3 and is also needed by the Shipwright — keep the Wood Cutters running even after the request is filled.
  • Libraries consume papyrus; budget production above the export quota or the culture rating will stall.
  • Behdet's reduced export list (fish and clay only) is scripted — do not plan an economy around its old goods.
  • The requests escalate in size; a full granary and stocked Storage Yards trivialize them, and each fulfilled request is +8 kingdom rating toward the goal of 40.

Mission script — src/scripts/mission/m_012_meidum.js

The entire mission — configuration block, variables, and all event handlers — lives in one file:

src/scripts/mission/m_012_meidum.js

This file is imported by src/scripts/missions.js. Key parts of the mission12 { … } block:

// lines 3 – 148  (m_012_meidum.js)
mission12 {
  start_message : "message_mission_meidum"
  player_rank : 1

  choice [                       // post-victory fork (line 14): same pair as mission 11
    { name : "Buhen",   id : 13 }
    { name : "Dahshur", id : 14 }
  ]

  // Goals from the Pharaoh Heaven walkthrough (original .pak values NOT yet verified):
  // pop 3000, culture 25, prosperity 25, monument 39, kingdom 40.
  // TODO(C1): restore goal 39 and swap medium stepped pyramid for the complex.

    population { enabled : true, goal : 3000 }

    prosperity { enabled : true, goal : 25 }

    kingdom    { enabled : true, goal : 40 }

  cities [ … ]                   // line 98 — trade partners, unverified vs .pak
}

The monument goal of 31 is exactly the rating produced by completing both available stepped pyramids: weights 8 (small) + 16 (medium) → 6.32 × √24 + 0.5 = 31 (see src/scripts/city/monuments.js). When the stepped pyramid complex (weight 44) is implemented, the goal reverts to the original 39.

Known deviations from the original (tracked)
  • Monument goal: original is 39 (small stepped pyramid + stepped pyramid complex); currently 31 (small + medium stepped pyramids) because the complex class is not implemented — TODO(C1) in the mission script, tracked as task C1 in REMAKE_TASKS_P1.md.
  • Serabit Khadim trade route: in the original it opens mid-mission after the Sinai expedition succeeds; there is no JS API to open a route yet, so it is available from the start.
  • Request years/amounts (except wood in year 3) and invasion timing/entry point are provisional, taken from walkthrough prose, not from the original mission data.
  • Trade partners and their goods are inferred (Behdet's decline is scripted per the briefing) — verify against the original empire map.
