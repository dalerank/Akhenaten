Briefing

The mission opens with the history panel (message_history_north_dahshur) and the briefing popup (message_mission_north_dahshur):

Pharaoh Snofru has brought order to Egypt, and the Kingdom now flourishes under his wise and benevolent rule. Snofru wishes to undertake another construction project at Dahshur, even more ambitious than the bent pyramid already completed. Royal architects are confident that they can construct an edifice whose sides rise in one continuous angle, culminating in a perfect peak. If successful, this will be the first true pyramid, and a worthy home for Pharaoh Snofru through all eternity!

Pharaoh's wife, Queen Hetepheres, has recently given birth to a son whom they have named "Khufu". Our people look toward the day of his eventual rule with much foreboding, for the seers of Horus have predicted that he will show none of his father's benevolence — though he will achieve many great things, they fear he will rule with unyielding tyranny.

You may acquire wood from Byblos, since carpenters will have to build many ramps to allow workers to reach the summit of this great pyramid.

North Dahshur is where Snofru built the Red Pyramid, the first successful true (smooth-sided) pyramid and the direct precursor to Khufu's Great Pyramid. In the campaign it is the payoff of the Snofru arc — the largest monument project yet — and it plants the seed of the Khufu missions to come. It is also a fork point: after completing it the player chooses Iunet or On.

Objectives

  • Population3 000 citizens
  • Culture rating20
  • Prosperity rating30
  • Monument rating58 — complete both stepped pyramids (a large true pyramid in the original)
  • Kingdom rating55
Temporary deviation from the original: the original requires one large true pyramid (monument rating 32). True pyramids are not implemented yet (task C3). As a stand-in the mission requires both the small and the medium stepped pyramid — a larger commitment than mission 14 — which yields a rating of 58 with the additive rating formula. When true pyramids land and the per-type weights are calibrated against the original (task F3), the goal reverts to 32 and a single large pyramid replaces the two stepped ones.

Scripted events

Pharaoh's requests keep the pyramid economy under pressure; two raids test the city's defenses. Amounts and timing are provisional (walkthrough prose, not the original data).

Year 2Pharaoh requests 18 grain (18 months). Completion +8 kingdom, refusal −6 — applies to each request.
Year 3Pharaoh requests 15 straw. In summer the Libyans invade from the north-west (14 units).
Year 4Pharaoh requests 14 barley.
Year 5Pharaoh requests 24 limestone. In late autumn the Bedouins raid from the west (12 units).

Strategy

North Dahshur combines the biggest monument so far with recurring raids from two directions and the highest prosperity target yet (30). The economy must feed the pyramid stone and limestone while keeping housing valuable and the granaries full for Pharaoh's grain/straw/barley requests.

Phase 1 — Broad food base

Grain and barley farms on the floodplain (with Water Lifts and Irrigation Ditches) feed 3 000 citizens and stock the granaries against Pharaoh's cereal requests. Barley also feeds Breweries; straw is the farming by-product Pharaoh asks for.

Phase 2 — The great pyramid

This is the largest build yet — both stepped pyramids stand in for the true pyramid. Stone Quarries and a Limestone Quarry plus multiple Work Camps and all three guilds keep construction moving. Import timber from Byblos for the ramps (carpenters), and plain stone from Men-nefer or Abu if local quarrying can't keep up.

Phase 3 — Defense from two sides

Libyans come from the north-west, Bedouins from the west — wall the city, keep a Military Academy and trained infantry/archer companies, and don't stand the army down between raids. Towers along the western and north-western approaches cover both entry directions.

Phase 4 — Prosperity 30

The prosperity goal is the highest so far. Evolve housing with luxury goods (jewelry from imported gems), good services, and beautification (statues, gardens, plazas). Hold festivals to keep the gods content while the pyramid drains the labor pool.

Tips

  • Start both stepped pyramids early — the monument goal (31) needs both completed; a single medium stepped only reaches 25.
  • Keep a timber buffer from Byblos — a carpenter shortage stalls the ramp/construction phases.
  • Stockpile cereals ahead of Pharaoh's requests; each fulfilled request is +8 kingdom toward the goal of 55.
  • The two raid directions (NW Libyans, W Bedouins) are close together — a western defensive line covers both.
