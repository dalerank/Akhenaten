Briefing

The mission opens with the history panel (message_history_buhen) and the briefing popup (message_mission_buhen):

Our new Pharaoh, Snofru, is determined that this, the fourth dynasty, be remembered as the greatest ever to rule Egypt. Pharaoh wishes that our borders be pushed even further south. He has therefore decreed that we invade Nubia, and establish a fortified city at Buhen, beside the second cataract of the Nile. There you must also erect a granite obelisk, to serve as a beacon that this area forever belongs to Egypt. There is no granite to be found so far south, however, so you will need to import it from Abu.

At Buhen you will encounter fierce and seasoned Nubian warriors. After rigorous training at a Military Academy, your fighting men may emerge as seasoned veterans themselves. Our military engineers have perfected the design of fortified Towers, Walls and Gatehouses. Transport ships may also be employed, to aid in moving your army around by water.

Pharaoh's burial place, a unique and spectacular monument, is currently under construction at Dahshur. From time to time Pharaoh may request that you contribute some limestone to aid in the completion of this project.

Buhen was a real Egyptian fortress at the second cataract of the Nile in Lower Nubia, one of a chain of mud-brick strongholds that guarded the southern frontier and the gold and trade routes. In the campaign it is the military branch of the Snofru fork: a walled garrison town that must survive repeated Nubian assaults while feeding limestone to Pharaoh's pyramid at Dahshur.

Temporary deviation from the original: the monument goal (9) is the value of the original small granite obelisk, which is not implemented yet (task C7). A small mastaba produces the same rating (9), so it stands in for the obelisk and the goal number is unchanged. When the obelisk is implemented it replaces the mastaba stand-in.

Strategy

Buhen is a fortress economy: a defended core that exports limestone to Pharaoh while fending off Nubians and answering a distant-battle call — all while pushing kingdom rating to a demanding 75.

Phase 1 — Settle behind walls

Pick a compact site and wall it early with mud walls, gatehouses, and towers. Hunting Lodges and floodplain grain feed the town; a Limestone Quarry is essential — it supplies both your own needs and Pharaoh's requests. Import granite from Abu (needed later for the obelisk) and copper from Enkomi or Serabit Khadim.

Phase 2 — A veteran army

Build a Recruiter, Weaponsmith, and a Military Academy, then raise infantry and archer companies and run them through the academy — trained veterans beat the seasoned Nubians. Keep one infantry and one archer company ready for the first raid (year 3) and expand for the larger second raid (year 6). A Transport Wharf lets you shuttle troops across the Nile, as the briefing suggests.

Phase 3 — The obelisk and the kingdom rating

Erect the monument (the granite obelisk once available; a mastaba in the meantime) — worth 9 monument points, enough for the goal. The real challenge is kingdom rating 75: fulfil every Pharaoh request, answer the distant battle, avoid loans, and send gifts from personal savings when the rating lags.

Tips

  • Keep a limestone buffer at all times — Pharaoh's pyramid requests recur and each fulfilled one is +8 kingdom rating.
  • Do not disband the army after the first raid; the second Nubian assault is larger.
  • Temples of Seth (god of the desert/war) help in a military mission — keep him appeased.
  • Copper feeds the Weaponsmith; secure a cheap supply from Serabit Khadim before prices can double from a failed distant battle.
