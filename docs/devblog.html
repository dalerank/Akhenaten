Game++. Cooking Vectors (Part 2)

Discusses hybrid_vector — a static_vector that falls back to heap allocation when exceeding a threshold. Draws a parallel to SSO (Short String Optimization). Covers when hybrid_vector helps vs. when it doesn't (e.g., large element sizes, no reasonable small N). Notes that C++17's std::pmr::monotonic_buffer_resource can emulate this pattern.

May 21, 2025

Game++. Cooking Vectors (Part 1)

Explores performance tradeoffs of std::vector in game dev:

  • Allocation costs — resize copies, fragmentation on consoles, wasted capacity
  • std::array — faster but fixed-size; can blow the stack (PS4→Switch porting anecdote with 480KB stack limit)
  • static_vector — stack-allocated vector with fixed max size; absent from C++ standard even in C++26
  • Links to the project's own implementation at src/core/svector.h
May 9, 2025

Game++. String Interning

Covers string interning (string pools) as a game engine optimization:

  • Compiler-level interning works on PlayStation (Clang/GCC) but "on Xbox, this trick won't work"
  • Fragile optimization — breaks with char[] or std::string copies
  • Custom xstring class using CRC hashes enables pointer-based comparison (30% animation perf boost cited)
  • Used to ship Sims Mobile on iPhone 4S when it originally targeted iPhone 6
  • Implementation at src/core/xstring.h
April 17, 2025

Should Players Be Hand-Held?

A game design essay analyzing level design techniques from Witcher 3, Zelda, and Metro Exodus:

  • Landmarks/Weenies — term coined by Walt Disney; always visible orientation points
  • Golden path — natural story progression via environmental cues
  • Breadcrumbs — light, color, glowing mushrooms guiding players
  • Walls — critique of invisible walls; preference for natural barriers
  • Affordance — interactive elements should look like what they do
  • Visual shapes — round=fragile, square=solid, triangle=dangerous
  • Leading lines — composition technique from painting directing player gaze

May 28, 2019

The Story of Original Pharaoh Game

A history of Pharaoh (1999) by Impressions Games:

  • Development began fall 1997; Caesar 3 engine was the foundation
  • Simon Bradbury (engine/code lead) left mid-development to found Firefly Studios
  • Chris Beatrice took over as lead; chose Ancient Egypt setting over "Caesar in space"
  • Haidi Mann led art using "ping-pong texturing" — 3D models redrawn as 2D tiles
  • Game sold 1.7 million copies at $45 each on a sub-$2M budget
  • Rights passed through multiple owners; Dotemu released Pharaoh: A New Era remake
  • Dalerank took over the open-source recreation after the original author abandoned it
May 28, 2019

How to Build a Mastaba

Technical deep-dive into recreating the mastaba monument system:

  • Original used composite building rendering in a single pass — extremely complex
  • New approach breaks mastaba into independent isometric parts (slanted wall, entrance, solid wall)
  • Covers the .sg3/.555 texture format (Sierra Graphics V3): uncompressed, compressed (RLE), and isometric packing
  • Construction has 8 stages: 2 site levelings + 6 stone-laying phases, requiring 400 stones per segment
  • Monuments acted as dynamic warehouses — resources appeared only when physically delivered
